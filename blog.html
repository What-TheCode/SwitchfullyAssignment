How to take a great picture

people

Choose your object

A typical photograph is made up of two basic elements: the subject and the environment in which it’s located. The subject’s position against the background, as well as the background’s own position, are just as important as the subject.
A good composition should have a clear main subject.

grid_3x3

Use the rule of thirds

The rule of thirds is one of most well-known compositional rules in photography, and describes a basic compositional structure of a picture. The frame can be split in 9 segments by using 3 vertical and 3 horizontal lines. The idea behind this concept is to place the subject on one of the 4 intersections of these lines. Although this is a widley used concpt, this is not a requirement to take a good picture in every situation!

light_mode

Look for great lighting

In order to take a great picture lighting is one of the most important things to keep in mind. One of the best times to take a picture is the golden hour. This is a timeframe just after en before sunset, and results in a golden-hued scene which makes for a warm picture.

camera

Use the correct exposure (exposure triangle)

There are three basics to master a good exposure:
Aperture can be defined by the opening in the lens through wich the light passes. A bigger opening lets in more light, and vice versa. Aperture can also add dimention and perspective to your photo by controlling the depth of field.
Shutter speeds is the amount of time the shutter is open. This is measured in seconds. You can use a fast shutter speed to freeze movement, or use a slow one to take a blurred shot. When shooting hendheld, a shutterspeed of more than 1/60th of a second is recommended in order to take a crisp shot.
And last but not least ISO. ISO basically is a measure of the camera's sensor ability to capture light. In poor lighting the ISO can be raised to capture more light, at the cost of a granier image.
These three are in close relationship to each other; when you adjust one of them, you have to consider adjusting the others to get the desired result. The automatic mode on the camera takes care of all these for you, but you pay the price of not getting the picture to look the way you want them to look.
